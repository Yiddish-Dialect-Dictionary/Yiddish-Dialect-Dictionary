    אָרעם 'poor' A3

    ETYMOLOGY

    Got. arms, ON armr, OS arm, OHG aram, NHG arm < PGmc. arma- 'miserable, pitiful, poor' < PIE h₃orbʰ-mo-; cf. Latin orbus, Greek ὀρφανός 'orphaned', Sanskrit árbha- 'small, young' {DW² 3: 264; Kluge/Seebold 2011: 60; Kroonen 2013: 35}

    WESTERN

    Orme Leut kochen mit Wasser. {Maißim un Schnokes vun e Handelewos, LEIPZIG 1845: 40} • En ormer Mon muß mit 'n Untrmon stachen. {?} {42} • Fun Nemin ward me nit orm. {43} • En ormer Mon darf kan חוצפה hobe. {48}

    arm {HOLLAND, Beem, Jerosche} • Gehot is 'n arm man. '"Had" is a poor man. For someone who talks about the past, about what he had. What one used to have does not help any more.' {Jerosche №236} • Wen e loch in die brik is, falt der arme man dorch. 'If there is a hole in the bridge, the poor man will fall through. Misfortune always happens to the unlucky one. Cf. 'n arm man is n' schlemiel.' {Jerosche №565}
