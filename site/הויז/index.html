    ETYMOLOGY

    MHG hûs

    haisər הײַזער {Schnitzler, Prager Judendeutsch, 21}

    hous {ɔu} {HOLLAND, Beem, Jerosche} • Wo hot 'n hond hous? 'Where does a dog have a house? What do you imagine?' {Jerosche №356} • Besser a leddeg hous wie e schleschte einwouner. {Jerosche №364} • Es hous verliert niks. Var. Was es hous verliert, gibt es hous widder. 'If something has been lost at home, it will eventually turn up. Don't spend too long looking for it.' {Jerosche №365} • In kaan jiddisch hous gedocht. 'Phrase used to ward off accidents.' {Jerosche №366} • Mit viel halt man hous, mit winzeg komt man ous. 'Met veel houdt men huis, met weinig komt men uit. With a lot of people one keeps a home, with little one comes out. Who has a lot can be generous. who has little, must see it come true. {???}' {Jerosche №367}
