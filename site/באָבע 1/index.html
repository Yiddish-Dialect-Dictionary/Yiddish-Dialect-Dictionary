    באָבע 1 די 'grandmother; midwife'

    ETYMOLOGY

    Beider suggests that the word for 'grandmother' is likely to come from the same source as for 'grandfather', which he believes is from Old Czech (see זיידע). Beider suggests that intimate family vocabulary is likely to come from the West Cnaanic (Old Czech) substrate. The long vowel in Czech (which merged with protovowel A₂ in EY) would also explain the development of the vowel in Y dialects (e.g., CY bubə, NEY bobə.) {The variation in the vowel suggests that the word was borrowed more than once from different Slavic languages/dialects.} Old Czech bába; cf. Old Polish baba. {Beider 2015: 431, 451, 455}
