    עטע דער 'father'

    ETYMOLOGY

    Got. atta, ON atti, OHG atto 'forefather', MHG atte, ätte 'forefather', ENHG ette, NHG (Swabian, Swiss dialects) ätti, (Bavarian) ätt < PGmc. attan- < PIE atta; cf. Hittite atta-, Greek ἄττα, Latin atta, Russian оте́ц, Polish ojciec Probably originally a nursery word. {AHDW 1: 689; Derksen 2007: 383; DW¹ 1: 596; EWA 1: 385; Feist 62; Kroonen 2013: 39}

    Ette from ätti (according to Kluge Et. W. schwaebisch-alemannisch), MHG Atte. Goetze Glossar: ette. Memme - Kluge: late MHG.
