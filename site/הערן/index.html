    הערן געהערט 'to hear' (NORTHEASTERN also 'to smell')

    ETYMOLOGY

    The greeting ?וואָס הערט זיך 'what's up?' appears to be a calque of Polish Co słychać? lit. 'what is heard?' The phrase איך הער דיך ווי אין טראָק 'I hear you as in Trakai (i.e., I do not listen to you, you are not my boss)' stems from the fact that Trakai was home to many Karaites. who did not listen to the rabbis. Another explanation: Jews in Trakai were protected by a privilegium. {לעמכען 1995: 33־34}
