    פֿיר 'four'

    ETYMOLOGY

    Got. fidwor, ON fjórir, OE fēower, English four, OS fior, Dutch vier, OHG fior, MHG vier, NHG vier < PGmc. fedwar- 'four' (West Germanic fewar) < PIE kʷét-uor-, from the root kʷet- 'angle' (?); Germanic has initial f- insteadof hw- due to the influence of fimfe 'five' (see פֿינף). Related Sanskrit catvā́ras, Greek τέσσαρες, Latin quattuor-, Old Church Slavonic četyre, Russian четыре, Lithuanian keturì {Burrow 77, 259; DW¹ 26: 251; Kluge/Seebold 2011: 960; Kroonen 2013: 133}
