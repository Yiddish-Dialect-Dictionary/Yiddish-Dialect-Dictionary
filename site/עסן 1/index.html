    עסן 1 געגעסן

    ETYMOLOGY

    {DW¹ notes that the past participle was originally gessen, from the 14th c. also geessen, and only from the second half of the 15th c. instances of gegessen - which does not come to dominate fully until the 19th c.} Got. itan, ON eta, OE etan, English to eat, OS etan, Dutch eten, OHG ezzan, MHG ezzen, (past participle) gezzen, NHG essen < < PGmc. etanaⁿ or etan- or et-a- 'to eat' < PIE h₁éd-. Related: Hittite ez(za)zi ~ adanzi, Sanskrit átti, (3pl.) adánti, Greek ἔδω, Latin edō, ēsse, Lithuanian ė́sti, Old Church Slavonic jasti, Russian есть < h₁éd-ti, (3pl.) h₁d-énti. Perhaps related to PIE dont-'tooth' (see צאָן), so the original meaning might have been 'to bite'. {DW¹ 8: 2398; Fulk 289; Kluge/Seebold 2011: 260; Kroonen 2013: 119; Lexer 1: 718} Vowel 21, Proto-Yiddish ɛ {Katz 1982: 58}
