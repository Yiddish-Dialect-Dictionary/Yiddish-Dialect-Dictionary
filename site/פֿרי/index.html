    פֿרי 'early' (adjective, adverb) פֿריִער 'earlier (comparative)'

    ETYMOLOGY

    Middle Low German vrō, Dutch vroeg, OHG fruo, MHG vruo, NHG früh < PGmc. frōi 'early' (or frō-) < PIE próH-i; from the root per- 'in front, early, first'; a petrified locative identical to Greek πρωί, πρῴ 'early in the morning'. From the adverb was derived the adjective *frōja-: OHG fruoi, MHG vrüe(je), NHG früh, (archaic) frühe, Yiddish פֿרי (more often פֿריִיִק). Related: Sanskrit prātár 'early', prá 'forward', Latin prō 'for'. {DW² 9: 1159; EWA 3: 605; Kluge/Seebold 2011: 321; Kroonen 2013: 156}
