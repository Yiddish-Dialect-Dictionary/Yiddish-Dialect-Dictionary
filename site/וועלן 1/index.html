    וועלן 1 'will (future auxiliary); to want'

    ETYMOLOGY

    Got. wiljan, ON vilja, OE willan, OS willian, wellian, OHG wellan, NHG wollen < PGmc. weljan- 'to want' < PIE uelh₂-; cf. Latin volō, velle, Lithuanian pa-vélti [Feist 563, Kroonen 2013: 578]

    WESTERN

    Me ward schaun hern, sagt holitscher שמש. {Maißim un Schnokes vun e Handelewos, LEIPZIG 1845: 41} • Wenn s tropt waß er s wat regnen. {45} • Wer nit will der muß. {47}

    wellen {HOLLAND, Beem, Jerosche} • Mit wellen ken men kaan hond ous es loch lokken. 'You can't lure a dog out of its hole by wanting (alone). The will alone is not enough, it comes down to the deed.' {Jerosche №354}

    velə 'to want' {ALSACE, Zuckerman 1969, 42}

    wer (first person singular); wert (third person singular) {HOLLAND, Beem, Jerosche} • Die bloue gritz wert im schmekken. 'The blue groats will be tasty for him. It is out with the fun. Now the hard life starts again.' {Jerosche №304} • Wen es gut wert sein, wer isch nit meer dou sein. {Jerosche №306} • Er wert sisch in s'n keiwer umdreien. 'Said of something that would have caused the deceased great grief.' {Jerosche №440}

    iç vɔr 'I will' {ALSACE, Zuckerman 1969, 43} • ɛr vɔrt 'he will'

    gəvelt געוועלט {WTCP, U. Weinreich 1964: 256}
