טריב
'dreary'

ETYMOLOGY
===========
OE drōf turbid; troubled', OS drōbi 'cloudy, sad', Dutch droef, OHG truobi 'turbid, dark, confused', MHG trüebe 'dim, gloomy' < PGm. *drōbu- 'turbid; troubled' (EUR) or *drōba-/ja- (Kroonen writes that it was originally a u-stem remodeled as a ja-stem in continental Germanic). Perhaps from PIE *dʰrebʰ- 'to fracture'; cf. Lithuanian drebėti 'to quiver', Russian дроба́ (see דרויב).
Related to *drabiz- 'dregs' (ON draf 'draff, husks').
Perhaps related to Polish drobny 'small, tiny' (see דראָבנע), Ukrainian дрібний (see דריבנע) ' < Proto-Slavic *drobьnъ 'small, fine'.
{Derksen 2007: 119; DW¹  22: 1165; Kluge/Seebold 2011: 932; Kroonen 2013: 103}
