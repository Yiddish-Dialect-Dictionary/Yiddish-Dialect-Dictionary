אויער
־ן
דער/דאָס
(NORTHEASTERN and SOUTHEASTERN דער)
'ear'

ETYMOLOGY
===========
Got. auso, ON eyra, OE ēare, English ear, OS ōra, OHG ōra, MHG ôre, ôr, NHG Ohr < PGmc. *auzōn- < PIE *h₂e/ous-n- < * h₂óus-s, gen. *h₂éus-s, du. h₂usih₁, from the root h₂eu- 'to perceive' (e.g., Greek ἀΐω); cf. Lithuanian ausìs, Old Church Slavonic uxo, Greek οὖς, Latn auris, 
{DW¹ 13: 1224; Kluge 2013: 667; Kroonen 2013: 44}
The idiom פֿאַרליבן זיך איבער די אויערן 'to fall in love over one's ears (i.e., head over heels)' appears to be a calque of Polish zakochać się po uszy. 
{Fliciński  508}
