ЦҫЧЎ
Ч“ЧўЧЁ
'finger'

ETYMOLOGY

Got. figgrs, ON fingr, OE finger, English finger, OS fingar, Dutch vinger, OHG fingar, MHG vinger, NHG Finger; from PGmc вңұfingra- (or вңұfengra-); from PIE вңұpenkК·-rГі-; a ro-stem derived from the number вңұfimfe (see ЧӨЦҝЧҷЧ ЧЈ).
{DWВІ 9: 509; Kluge/Seebold 2011: 295; Kroonen 2013: 140}
The idiom ЧҗЧ•ЧҷЧЈ Ч“Чҷ ЧӨЦҝЧҷЧ Ч’ЧўЧЁ ЧӨЦҝЧ•Чҹ ЧҗЧҷЧҷЧҹ Ч”ЧҗЦ·Ч Чҳ ЧҗЧ•ЧҷЧЎЧҰЧ•ЧҰЧҷЧҷЧңЧҹ 'can be counted on the fingers of one hand (i.e., few)' apperas to be a calque of Polish mozМҮna/mГіc policzycМҒ cosМҒ/kogosМҒ na palcach jednej reМЁki.
{FliciЕ„ski 208}
The idiom ЧңЧўЧ§Чҹ Ч“Чҷ ЧӨЦҝЧҷЧ Ч’ЧўЧЁ (ЧӨЦҝЧ•Чҹ ЧўЧӨЦјЧўЧЎ) 'to lick one's fingers (i.e. to enjoy; finger-lickin' good)' appears to be a calque of Polish palce lizaДҮ.
