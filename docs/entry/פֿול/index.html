פֿול
'full'

ETYMOLOGY

{Why the vowel /u/? It looks like the MHG form was almost always vol (though Diefenbach also lists ful.) A shame there is no Wenker map.}
Got. fulls, ON fullr, OE full, English full, OS ful(l), fol, Dutch vol, OHG fol, (Tatian - just once! - acc. pl. fulle), MHG vol, NHG voll; from PGmc ✱fulla-; from PIE ✱plh₁-nó- (or ✱pḷəno- 'filled'), an adjective from the original past participle of the verbal root ✱pelh₁- 'to fill' (or ✱pelə-/plē-) (see פֿיל).
Related: Sanksrit pūrṇá-, Lithuanian pìlnas, Old Church Slavonic plĭnŭ 'filled, full', Latin plēnus,
