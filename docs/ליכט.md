ליכט
דאָס
(NORTHEASTERN די = mass gender and intermediate gender)
'light; candle'

ETYMOLOGY
===========
The idiom זוכן מיט ליכט 'to search for something rare, difficult to find' appears to be a calque of Polish szukać ze świecą / świeczką. 
{Fliciński 406}

Got. liuhaþ, OE lēoht, English light, OS lioht, OHG lioht, NHG Licht < PGmc. *leuhada- < PIE *leuk-ot-, old t-stem from the root *leuk- 'to shine'; cf. Hittite lukkatt- 'dawn', Latin lūx, Old Church Slavonic luča, Lithuanian laũkas
{EWA 5, 1351, Feist 334, Kroonen 2013: 333}
MHG lieht, OHG lioht, from Proto-Indo-European leuk- 'to shine,' the same root behind Greek λευκός (leukós), Latin lūx, Russian луч (luč), and English light. {DWDS}
