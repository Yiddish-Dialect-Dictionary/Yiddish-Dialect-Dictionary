ETYMOLOGY
===========
ON fjǫðr, OE feðer, English feather, OS fethera Dutch veer, OHG fedara, federa, MHG veder(e), NHG Feder' < PGmc. *feþrō- 'feather' (or *fed(u)rō) < PIE *péth₂-r/n-; heteroclitic derived from PIE root *peth₂- 'to fly'.
Related: Hittite pattar ~ pattan-, pettar ~ pettan- 'feather, wing', Sanskrit pátra- 'id', Greek πτερόν 'wing', Latin penna 'feather'.
