פֿול
'full'

ETYMOLOGY
===========
{Why the vowel /u/? It looks like the MHG form was almost always vol (though Diefenbach also lists ful.) A shame there is no Wenker map.}
Got. fulls, ON fullr, OE full, English full, OS ful(l), fol, Dutch vol, OHG fol, (Tatian - just once! - acc. pl. fulle), MHG vol, NHG voll < PGmc. *fulla- < PIE *plh₁-nó- (or *pḷəno- 'filled'), an adjective from the original past participle of the verbal root *pelh₁- 'to fill' (or *pelə-/plē-) (see פֿיל).
Related: Sanksrit pūrṇá-, Lithuanian pìlnas, Old Church Slavonic plĭnŭ 'filled, full', Latin plēnus,
{BMZ 3: 361; Diefenbach 1857: 442; DW¹ 26: 515; EWA 3: 445; Kluge/Seebold 2011: 962; Kroonen 2013: 159; Lexer 3: 432}
