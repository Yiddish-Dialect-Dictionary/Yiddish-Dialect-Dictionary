קינד
־ער
דאָס
(NORTHEASTERN דער/די, mainly דער, rarely also די סקינד—the last remnant of the neuter)
'child'

ETYMOLOGY
===========
Vowel 31, Proto-Yiddish *i
{Katz 1982: 58}
The idiom אײַנרעדן אַ קינד אין בויך 'to convince that one has a child in one's belly (i.e., to delude, take for a ride)' appears to be a calque of Polish wmówić dziecko w brzuch.
{Fliciński 462}
