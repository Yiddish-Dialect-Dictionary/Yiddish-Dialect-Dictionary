ETYMOLOGY
===========
{Why is the vowel /i/ instead of /u/ as in NHG? Probably this is not the original noun, but derived secondarily from the verb פֿיטערן.}
Got. fodr 'scabbard, sheath, casing', ON fóðr, OE fōdor, English fodder, Dutch voeder, OHG fuotar, MHG vuoter, NHG Futter < PGmc. *fōdra- 'fodder' < PIE *peh₂-dʰro- (or *pā-tro-, from the root *pā-t-, *pə-t- 'feed, graze, nourish', or from *peh₂-(i)- 'protect, guard, graze'); related to *fōdjan- 'to feed' (OE fēdan, English to feed); 
Related: Latin pābulum 'fodder'
