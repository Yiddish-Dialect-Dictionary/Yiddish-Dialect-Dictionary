געפֿינען
געפֿונען
'to find'

ETYMOLOGY
===========
{The reflexive געפֿינען זיך 'to be located' is perhaps a calque of the Polish znajdować się - although the German is almost identical: sich befinden.}
{Why the prefix ge- and why the loss of -d-?
Wenker Sprachatlas map 453 shows that ge- in the past participle is mainly Central German, more specifically East Central German; Upper German dialects largely have g-, and Low German has no prefix at all. Sadly no map for the rest of the word.}
Got. finþan, ON finna, OE findan, English to find, OS findan, Dutch vinden, OHG findan, MHG vinden, NHG finden < PGmc. *finþan- 'to find; to feel' (or *fenþ-a-) < PIE *pént-e-; perhaps a nasalization of the PIE root *pet- 'to fly, to fall'; or from the root *pent(h) 'to go, step, find'; or *pent- 'to find a way'.
Related: Sanskrit pánthās, gen. pathás 'road, path, course', Greek πάτος 'road', πόντος 'sea', Latin pōns 'bridge, path', Old Church Slavonic pǫtĭ 'road'
{DW² 9: 501; Kluge/Seebold 2011: 295; Kroonen 2013: 141}
