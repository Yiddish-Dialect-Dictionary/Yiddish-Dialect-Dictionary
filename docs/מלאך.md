WESTERN
========

Malach, Malochim 'Engel' {PhilogLottus 1733}

Mallech; meloochem {HOLLAND, Beem, Jerosche}
	•	Wen man die kalle zur choppe toot fieren, schtein die meloochem hinter die tieren. 'Relates to the custom that blood relations lead bride and groom under the bridal canopy. {Jerosche №427}
	•	S'n mallech is e fresscher. 'His angel is a pig (glutton). He always comes when there is something tasty on the table (thanks to his guardian angel). Said of someone who loves good food. Fresscher < German Fresser.' {Jerosche №591}
Tendlau 529.
	•	Saan mallech is e gescht. 'His angel is a beggar.' {Jerosche №592}
	•	Saan mallech is kaan gescht. 'The opposite of the previous number. Approximately: He is not disappointed in his positive expectations.' {Jerosche №592}
Tendlau 239.
	•	Mallech hagouel. E mallech hagouel hot mir angestoussen (Basterdized to: e mallech geigeltje). 'A redeeming angel has struck me. I got lucky. Mallech hagoueil = malach hago-el = redeeming angel. Citation from Genesis XLVIII, 16. The night prayer for children begins with this text.' {Jerosche №594}
