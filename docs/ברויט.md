ETYMOLOGY
===========
Crimean Got. broe, ON brauð, OE brēad, English bread, OS brōd, OHG brōt, MHG brôt, < PGmc. *brauda- 'bread' < *bʰrouh₁-tó- (DRV); a to-stem derived from *brewwen- 'to brew' (see ברויען). 
The word displaced the earlier Germanic word for 'bread' *hlaiba- (see לאַבן, English loaf).
{DW² 5: 846; EWA 2: 359; Kluge/Seebold 2011: 153; Kroonen 2013: 74}
Vowel 42, Proto-Yiddish *ō
{Katz 1982: 59, 286}
