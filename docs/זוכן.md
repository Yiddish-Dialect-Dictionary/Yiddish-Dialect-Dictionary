זוכן
געזוכט
'to seek, look for, search'

ETYMOLOGY
===========
Got. sokjan, ON sœkja, OE sēcan, English to seek, OS sōkian, OHG suohhen, NHG suchen < PGmc. *sōkjan- 'to seek, find, demand' < PIE *seh₂g-ie-; cf. Latin sāgīre 'to perceive', Greek ἡγέομαι 'to direct, lead'
[Feist 442, Kroonen 2013: 464] 
Vowel 52, Proto-Yiddish *ū
{Katz 1982: 59}
The idiom זוכן מיט ליכט 'to search for something rare, difficult to find' appears to be a calque of Polish szukać ze świecą / świeczką. 
{Fliciński 406}
The idiom !גיי זוך דעם ווינט אין פֿעלד appears to be a calque of Polish szukaj wiatru w polu.
{Fliciński 406}
