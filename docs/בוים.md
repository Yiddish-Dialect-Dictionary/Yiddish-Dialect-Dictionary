ETYMOLOGY
===========
Got. bagms 'tree', ON baðmr 'tree; beam', OE bēam 'tree, pillar, beam, post', English beam, OS bōm, OHG boum, MHG boum, NHG Baum < West Germanic *baumaz < PGmc. *bagma- 'beam, tree' < PIE bʰh₂ǵʰ-mo-
Probably related to PGmc. *bōgu- 'shoulder, upper arm' (e.g., OE bōg 'shoulder, arm; branch', English bough)
{DW² 4: 327;Kluge/Seebold 2011: 97; Kroonen 2013: 47}
Vowel 44, Proto-Yiddish *ɔu
{Katz 1982: 61}
{interesting that Katz writes the word בוֹים in his Litvish Bible translations, which indicates that it is to be pronounced /oj/ and /ej/, as if from U4, not O4.}
