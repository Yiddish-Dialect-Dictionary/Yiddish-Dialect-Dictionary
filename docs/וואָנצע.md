וואָנצע, ־ס/וואָנצן
די
וואָנס(ע‏)
'half mustache (sg.); mustache (pl.)'

ETYMOLOGY
===========
Polish wąsy (plural) 'mustache'; cf. Ukrainian вуса; Belarusian вусы; Russian усы́; from Proto-Slavic *vǫsъ, from *ǫ̃sъ; from PIE *wondʰ-s-om, from the root *wendʰ- 'hair, wool, beard' {?}; cf. OHG wintbrāwa 'eye-lash' (see ווימפּער).
{Derksen 2007: 386; Gajek 2023a: 9; Herzog 1964: 110; Kroonen 2013: 76}

![](https://ia902902.us.archive.org/9/items/Yiddish-Dialect-Maps/Herzog3-52-55-ToilHorseCollarNavelMustache-81.jpg)
There is notably no variant from East Slavic (without the nasal) even East of Polish linguistic territory. The Eastern variants with /n/ and /c/ show the "de-Polonization" of the loan word in the direction of native Yiddish patterns.
{Herzog 1964: 110}
