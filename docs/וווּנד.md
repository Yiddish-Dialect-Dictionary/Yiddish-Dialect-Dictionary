וווּנד
־ן
די
'wound'

ETYMOLOGY
===========
Got. wunds 'wounded', ON und 'wound', OE wund 'wounded'; wund 'wound', OS wund 'wounded'; wunda 'wound', OHG wunt 'wounded'; wunta 'wound', MHG wunde, NHG Wunde < PGmc. *wundō- 'wound', *wunda- 'wounded' < DRV *un-tó-; an adjective from a past participle of PGmc. *winnan- 'to suffer' (see געווינען). Cf. Lithuanian votìs 'open sore'
