טאָכטער
די
'daughter'

ETYMOLOGY
===========
Got. dauhtar, ON dóttir, OE dohtor, dohter, English daughter, OS dohtar, Dutch dochter, OHG tohter, MHG tohter, tochter, NHG Tochter < PGmc. *duhter- 'daughter' < PIE *dʰugh₂-ter-; Sanskrit duhitár-, Greek θυγάτηρ, Lithuanian duktė̃, Old Church Slavonic dŭšti, Hieroglyphic Luwian tuwatari.
Umlaut in the plural became evident only in late MHG (töhtere as well as tohtere, tohtre). {From the citations in the FNHDW, it looks like even ENHD more often lacked umlaut in the plural.}
{BMZ 3: 44; DW¹ 21: 532; FNHDW "tochter"; Kluge/Seebold 2011: 919; Kroonen 2013: 107; Lexer 2: 1457}
Vowel 41, Proto-Yiddish *ɔ
{Katz 1982: 58}
