מיך
'me (accusative)'

ETYMOLOGY
===========
Got. mik, ON mik, OE (Anglian) mec, English (oblique) me, OS (rare) mik, (oblique) mī, Dutch (oblique) mij, OHG mih, NHG mich < PGmc. *meki < PIE *(e)me-ǵe, an intensification of the normal unstressed accusative PIE *(e)me, as in Greek emége, Hittite ammuk. The particle -ǵe is associated specifically with the accusative only in Germanic.
{Kluge/Seebold 2011: 620; Kroonen 2013: 116}

WESTERN
========

Dutch Yiddish (like Northeastern Yiddish) lacks the forms מיך and דיך and uses מיר and דיר instead. {HOLLAND, Beem, Jerosche} {contradicted below}
