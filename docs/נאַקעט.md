נאַקעט
'naked'

ETYMOLOGY
===========
MHG nacket, nackent; OHG nackot; from PGmc. *nakwada-; cf. Got naqaþs; ON nǫkkviðr; OE nacod; English naked; from PIE *nogʷ-; cf. Lithuanian núogas; Latin nūdus; Hittite nekumant-; Sanskrit nāgá 'elephant (lit. the hairless one)'.
{DW¹ 13: 244; Kluge/Seebold 2011: 646}
