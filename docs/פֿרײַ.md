פֿרײַ
'free'

ETYMOLOGY
===========
{Interesting is the meaning 'secular', one of the primary meanings in Yiddish.
DW² meaning 2 c: "nicht an bestimmte (ethische, gesellschaftliche) regeln, normen gebunden, sich nicht danach richtend; häufig negativ bewertet" - with a citation from Wolfram von Eschenbach!}
Got. freis, OE frēo, frī, English free, OS frī, Dutch vrij, OHG frī, MHG vrî, NHG frei (in East Switzerland still meaning 'dear, friendly') < PGmc. *fri(j)a- 'free' < PIE * priH-o- (or *prii̯ō-, or *prih₂ó-); from the root *per(ə)i- 'near, by' (or from *prā-i-, prah₂-i̯-, prī-, prih₂- 'to like, spare'; related to *fri(j)ōn- 'to love' (see פֿרײַנד); the meaning 'free' arose from 'related', which points to a clan-based social system (Kroonen).
Related: ON Frigg (Odin's wife, 'the dear one' = OHG Friia, see פֿרײַטיק), Welsh rhydd 'free', Sanskrit priyá- 'dear', Avestan friia- 'dear, friendly', Latin proprius 'own', Russian прия́тный 'pleasant'
{DW² 9: 909; EWA 3: 555; Kluge/Seebold 2011: 315; Kroonen 2013: 155}
