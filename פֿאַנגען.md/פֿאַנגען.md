פֿאַנגען
געפֿאַנגען
'to capture'

ETYMOLOGY
===========
Got. fāhan, ON fǎ, (later) fanga, OE fōn, English (dialect) fang 'to catch', OS fāhan, Dutch vangen, OHG fâhan (fiang, fiangum, gifangan), MHG vâhen, vân, (sg.) vienc, vie, (pl.) viengen, (part.) gevangen, gevân), NHG fangen < PGmc. *fanhan- 'to catch' (or *fanh-a-) < *pónḱ-e- (NEUR) (or *pənḱ-); a univerbation of the adverb *h₂po and the root *h₁enḱ- 'to obtain' (or *h₂enḱ- 'to reach'). Perhaps originally 'to catch fish'. 
{DW² 9: 131; Kluge/Seebold 2011: 276; Kroonen 2013: 128}
