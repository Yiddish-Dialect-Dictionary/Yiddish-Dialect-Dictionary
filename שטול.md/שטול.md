שטול
־ן
די/דער
(CENTRAL די)
'chair'

ETYMOLOGY
===========
Got. stols, ON stōll, OE OS stōl, OHG stuol; cf. Old Church Slavonic stol, Lithuanian stãlas 'table', 
The original masculine gender was retained in NEY, while the new feminine gender emanated from the South.
{Herzog 1964: 161}
The idiom זיצן אויף צוויי שטולן 'to sit on two chairs (i.e., be two-faced; benefit from both sides)' appears to be a calque of Polish siedzieć na dwóch stołkach.
{Fliciński 369}
