ETYMOLOGY
===========
Used as a form of address (quasi-vocative) of רבֿ, before being adapted by Hasidim as the title of their guru-like charismatic leaders.
{Katz 2010: 123}

M. Weinreich suggests that the vowel /e/ in רבי is the result of umlaut (i-mutation), which must have taken place during the Old High German period while primary umlaut was still operative. This would be the earliest datable evidence of fusion in Yiddish. {M. Weinreich, Geshikhte, 1: 24}
