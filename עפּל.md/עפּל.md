עפּל
'apple'

ETYMOLOGY
===========
Crimean Gothic apel (< Got. *apl(u)s), ON epli (with secondary umlaut, < *apljan-), OE æpl, appel, English apple, OS appul, OHG apful, apfel, (pl.) epfili, NHG Apfel, (pl.) Äpfel < West Germanic *app(a)la- or *app(u)lu- < PGmc. *apla- < *h₂éb-ol- (EUR); cf. Lithuanian obuolỹs, Russian яблоко, Polish jabłko
Perhaps borrowed from a non-IE language.
The umlaut was occasionally extended to the singular already in OHG, becoming more common in late MHG (epfel), and it remains in some Upper German dialects (epfl). 
{BMZ 1: 49; DWB² 3: 133; Kroonen 2013: 31; Lexer 1: 87}
