ETYMOLOGY
===========
{Interesting that Eastern Yiddish has adopted the NHG form with final -n, rather than apocopating the MHG and ENHG form balke to **balk. Perhaps this is a wider pattern of weak nouns acquiring a final -n in NHG and Eastern Yiddish?}
ON bjalki,  bǫlkr, OE balca, bolca, English balk, OS balko, OHG balko, balcho 'plank, winepress, part of ship', MHG balke, ENHG balke, NHG Balken 'beam, bar' < PGmc. *belkan- ~ *balkan- ~ *bulkan- 'beam' < *bʰélǵʰ-on- (NEUR) < PIE *bʰelǵ-; cf. Russian (dialectal) bólozno 'thick board', Lithuanian balžíenas 'cross-beam'; Greek φᾰ́λᾰγξ 'line of battle', Latin fulciō 'to prop up, support'
{DW² 4: 92; Kluge/Seebold 2011: 85; Kroonen 2013: 58}

![](https://ia902902.us.archive.org/9/items/Yiddish-Dialect-Maps/Herzog3-76-Ceiling-93.jpg)
Herzog notes that באַלקן and its variants with the meaning 'ceiling' was popular mostly in the westernmost part of CY, closer to German territory. Further East, various Slavisms were used instead.  
{Herzog 1964: 127}

ETYMOLOGY
===========
ON bjalki (< PGmc. *belkᵏan-), OE bealca, English balk, bawk, OS balko, Dutch balk, OHG balko, balcho, MHG balke, NHG Balken < PGmc. *balkᵏan- 'beam' < PIE *bʰolǵʰ-, an expanded version of the root *bhel-, which is also behind Greek φάλκης (phálkēs) 'beam' and φάλαγξ (phálanx) 'roller, beam, battle line.' Original paradigm may have been PGmc. *belkᵏō, gsg. *bulkᵏaz, apl. *balkᵏuns, from older *bʰélǵʰ-ōn, *bʰl̥ǵʰ-n-ós, *bʰolǵʰ-n-ń̥s
{DWDS; Kroonen 2011: 148}
