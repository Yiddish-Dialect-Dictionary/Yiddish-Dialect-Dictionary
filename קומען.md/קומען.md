קומען
געקומען
'to come'

ETYMOLOGY
===========
Proto-Eastern Yiddish /ˈkumən/ stems from the Upper German variant in /u/ <kummen> (similarly פֿרום, גענומען, געשוווּמען).
{M. Weinreich, Geshikhte, 2: 85-86}
Got. qiman, ON koma, OE cuman, OS kuman, OHG queman < PGmc. *kʷem- < PIE *gʷém-e/o- (aorist subjunctive); Sanskrit gam- gácchati, Greek βαίνω‎, Latin venio
{Burrow 75; Fulk 255, 288}
Vowel 51, Proto-Yiddish *u
{Katz 1982: 58}
The idiom קומען צו זיך 'to come to oneself (i.e., recover, get well)' appears to be a calque of Polish dojść do siebie.
