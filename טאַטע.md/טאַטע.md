ETYMOLOGY
===========
{The EY form (usually with short /a/) probably comes from Polish tata, while the WY (or Czech Y) with long /aː/ is probably from Czech táta.}
Beider suggests that intimate family vocabulary is likely to come from the West Cnaanic (Old Czech) substrate. 
Polish tata; cf. Czech táta; Ukrainian та́то; Belarusian та́та; < Proto-Balto-Slavic *tatas < PIE *tatás; cf. Vedic Sanskrit tatá, Greek (vocative) τατᾶ, (Homer) τέττα, Latin tata, Romanian tată, Lithuanian tė̃tis, Russian тя́тя, NHG Tatta, tatte, tate {in DW¹!}

PHRASEOLOGY
טאַטע אין הימל
'father in heaven! (vocative)', translation of the Hebrew אָבִינוּ שֶׁבַּשָּׁמַיִם
Coincidentally matches the ancient Indo-European name for the sky god, PIE *di̯ḗu̯s ph₂tḗr, cf. Latin Jūpiter, Diēspiter, Sanskrit djáuṣ pitā́, Greek (vocative) Ζεῦ πάτερ
