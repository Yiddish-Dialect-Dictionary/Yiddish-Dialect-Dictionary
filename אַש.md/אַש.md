ETYMOLOGY
===========
Got. azgo 'ashes', ON aska, OE æsche, asce, axe, OHG asca 'ashes, dust', MHG asche, esche (secondary umlaut caused by the following /ʃ/), NHG Asche < PGmc. *askōn- 'ashes' < PIE h₂ed-dʰ-gʷʰ-eh₂-(?) (compound of *h₂ed- 'to dessicate' + *dʰegʷʰ- 'to burn'); or perhaps from PIE *azg- < *h₂es- 'to be hot, dry'; cf. Latin āridus 'dry', Sansrit ā́sa- 'ashes, dust', 
Perhaps from the root *has- 'hearth'.
{EWA 1: 364; Kluge/Seebold 2011: 64; Kroonen 2013: 38}
