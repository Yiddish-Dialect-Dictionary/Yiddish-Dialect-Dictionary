ETYMOLOGY
===========
Got. guþ )neuter!), ON goð, guð (m./n.), OE god, English god OS god, OHG got, NHG Gott < PGmc. *guda- < PIE *ĝʰu-tó- 'sacrifice (libation)' (?), from the root *ĝʰeu- 'to pour' (see גיסן).
Originally neuter and mostly used in the plural for gods in a collective sense: "the powers"
{EWA 4, 534, Feist 227, Kroonen 2013: 193, Strid 743}
The idiom איין גאָט ווייס 'God only knows, who knows' appears to be a calque of Polish Bóg jeden wie.
The idiom אָנכאַפּן גאָט פֿאַר די פֿיס 'to grab God by the feet (i.e., to be happy over success)' appears to be a calque of Polish  chwycić / trzymać / złapać Pana Boga za nogi.
[Fliciński 55, 427, 531] 
The idiom אָנכאַפּן גאָט פֿאַר דער באָרד 'to grab God by the beard (i.e., to be happy over success)' appears to be a calque of Belarusian схапіць / схапіць Бога за бараду
