פֿיר
'four'

Got. fidwor, ON fjórir, OE fēower, English four, OS fior, Dutch vier, OHG fior, MHG vier, NHG vier < PGmc. fedwar- 'four' (West Germanic fewar) < PIE kʷét-uor-, from the root kʷet- 'angle' (?); Germanic has initial f- insteadof hw- due to the influence of fimfe 'five' (see פֿינף).
Related Sanskrit catvā́ras, Greek τέσσαρες, Latin quattuor-, Old Church Slavonic četyre, Russian четыре, Lithuanian keturì
